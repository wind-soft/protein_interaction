User Guide

1. Enter Protein Information

On the homepage form, enter the names of two proteins in the fields labeled "Protein 1 Name" and "Protein 2 Name". Ensure that the entered information is accurate.

2. Calculate Docking Score

Click the "Calculate Docking Score" button. The system will perform the docking score calculation between the two proteins. Wait for a moment, and once the calculation is complete, the results will be displayed.

3. View Calculation Results

Once the calculation is complete, you will see the following information:

  • Docking Score: The specific numerical value representing the docking score. A higher value indicates a stronger interaction.
  • Interaction Result: Detailed interpretation of the docking score, including possible interaction types and binding strengths.
  • Interaction Plot: A graphical representation of the interaction information, providing a visual understanding of the binding situation.

4. Explore Calculation Steps

If you wish to explore each calculation step, navigate to the "Energy Calculation Steps" section. It contains detailed information for each step, including step number, protein pairing, and energy differences.

5. Recalculate

To initiate a new calculation, click the "Show Calculator" button, re-enter protein information, and then click "Calculate Docking Score" again.

Data Output Explanation

1. Docking Score

The Docking Score is a specific numerical value representing the strength of interaction between the proteins. A higher score indicates a more significant interaction.

2. Interaction Result

The Interaction Result provides a detailed explanation of the Docking Score, including possible interaction types and binding strengths.

3. Interaction Plot

The Interaction Plot is a graphical representation illustrating the spatial structure of protein interactions, offering a clear visualization of the binding scenario.

4. Energy Calculation Steps

The Energy Calculation Steps table details each calculation step, including step number, protein pairing, and energy differences. These data help in understanding the computational process behind the Docking Score.
